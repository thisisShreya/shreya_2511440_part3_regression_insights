# Residual Analysis — Multiple Regression Model (Model 3)

## Overview

Residuals are calculated as:

> **Residual = Actual Monthly Sales − Predicted Monthly Sales**

A positive residual means the model **under-predicted** (actual was higher than expected).  
A negative residual means the model **over-predicted** (actual was lower than expected).

The final multiple regression model (R² = 0.8145) was used for all predictions.

---

## Residual Calculation Method

Predicted sales were generated by applying the regression equation:

```
monthly_sales = 114,227.28
              + 34.12 × footfall
              + 1.20 × marketing_spend
              + 2,541.14 × inventory_availability_pct
              − 45,050.80 × avg_discount_pct
              + 11,283.11 × customer_rating
              + 12,611.58 × region_North
              + 23,827.80 × region_South
              + 19,762.80 × region_West
```

Reference categories: **East** (region), **Residential** (store_type)

### Business Interpretation — Positive Residuals

These stores are **outperforming** what the model predicted based on measurable input factors. Possible explanations include:

- **Mall and Airport stores** (STR-1028, STR-1026, STR-1051, STR-1064) may benefit from **captive audiences**, impulse buying, and high dwell time that footfall counts do not fully capture. The model does not include store type as a predictor, which may be a gap.
- **STR-1073 (Residential, East)** is significantly outperforming — this may indicate strong local brand loyalty, a skilled store manager, a particularly loyal customer base, or proximity to a key demographic cluster not captured in the data.
- These stores may have **unobserved competitive advantages** — e.g. absence of nearby competitors, better store layout, or superior product mix.
- Leadership should **investigate and replicate** the practices of these high-residual stores across the network.

### Business Interpretation — Negative Residuals

These stores are **underperforming** relative to model expectations. Possible explanations include:

- **STR-1017 (High Street, West)** has the largest negative residual. High Street stores typically have high footfall numbers but may face intense competition, high operational costs, or congestion reducing actual conversion rates. The model may overestimate sales from footfall in a high-competition zone.
- **STR-1012 and STR-1009 (Residential)** underperform despite decent input characteristics. This could reflect poor local management, higher staff turnover, a poorly performing product mix, or unrecorded local competition.
- **STR-1023 (Mall, South)** underperforms — Mall stores generally benefit from shared footfall, but internal store placement (e.g. near an exit, poor signage) could suppress sales.
- **STR-1077 (Residential, South)** has consistently lower-than-expected sales — may need operational review or investment.

---

## Patterns in Residuals

| Pattern | Observation |
|---------|-------------|
| **Store Type Bias** | Mall and Airport stores have several large positive residuals; High Street stores appear in the negative residual list. The model may be systematically underestimating Airport/Mall sales and overestimating High Street sales. |
| **Regional Patterns** | West region stores appear in the negative residuals, suggesting the model's regional dummies may not fully capture West-specific challenges (e.g., higher cost base, local economic factors). |
| **Potential Omitted Variables** | Store size, local income levels, competitor intensity, and store age are not in the dataset. Their absence likely drives residuals. |

---

## Model Bias Assessment

- The model does **not systematically over- or under-predict** across all stores — residuals range from approximately −£155,500 to +£127,400.
- However, there is **evidence of structural bias by store type**: Airport and Mall stores are under-predicted; High Street stores are over-predicted.
- Including `store_type` as a dummy variable in a future model iteration would likely reduce these residuals and improve R².

---

## Recommendation from Residual Analysis

1. **Investigate high-negative-residual stores** (STR-1017, STR-1012, STR-1023) for operational issues, competitive pressures, or data quality problems.
2. **Learn from high-positive-residual stores** (STR-1028, STR-1051) — identify what drives their outperformance and consider replicating those practices.
3. **Extend the model** to include `store_type` dummy variables and local competition intensity to reduce systematic residual bias.
